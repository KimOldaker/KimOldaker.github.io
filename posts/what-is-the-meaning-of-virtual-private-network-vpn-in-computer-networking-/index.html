2. How VPN Works?#

Virtual Private Network (VPN) is a secure way of connecting two or more endpoints, such as computers or other network devices, together in order to provide secure access to resources. VPNs use encryption, authentication, and tunneling protocols to offer a secure connection to data, applications, and networks. They are most commonly used to connect employees, customers, and partners to a corporate network. VPNs also offer users a way to access content that may not be available in their own country.

A VPN works by using a “tunnel” to encrypt data as it travels from one endpoint to the other, hiding it from anyone who attempts to intercept it. The tunnel is the “pipe” that carries the data between the two endpoints. Each tunnel consists of two parts: the tunnel endpoints and the tunneling protocol. The tunnel endpoints are the IP addresses of the computers at each endpoints. The tunneling protocol is the method used to encrypt the data and establish the secure connection.

The data is encrypted as it travels through the tunnel, preventing any attackers from intercepting it and using it for malicious purposes. On the other side of the tunnel, the data is decrypted and passed along to the destination. Once the data reaches its destination, the encryption process is reversed and the data is decrypted for the recipient to use.
