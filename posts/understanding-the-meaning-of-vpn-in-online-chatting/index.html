Q2. Why do people use a VPN? A2. People use a VPN for a variety of reasons. The most common reason is to access content that is restricted to their country or region. Other common uses include protecting personal data from unauthorized access and bypassing government internet censorship.

Q3. What are the benefits of using a VPN? A3. Some of the benefits of using a VPN include increased privacy and security, unrestricted access to content regardless of geographical location, access to public Wi-Fi networks without the risk of exposing sensitive data, as well as bypassing government internet censorship.

Q4. What are the risks of using a VPN? A4. The risks of using a VPN include potential privacy violations, slower connection speeds, the possibility of malicious software or viruses being installed, as well as the potential for government agencies to track activity on the VPN.
