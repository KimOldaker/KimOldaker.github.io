1. Definition and characteristics of Virtual Private Network (VPN)#

A Virtual Private Network (VPN) is an online service that allows users to establish a secure connection to another computer network over the internet. This connection is often used to anonymize a user’s web traffic and circumvent geographic restrictions. By using a VPN, a user can communicate with remote networks as if they were connected directly to the local network.

The main characteristics of a VPN are privacy and security. All data sent over a VPN connection is encrypted, usually with strong algorithms like AES. This encryption ensures that no third-party can intercept the traffic, such as an ISP, government, or hacker. VPNs also protect a user’s privacy by masking their true IP address. This prevents anyone from tracking a user’s online activities.

Another important characteristic of a VPN is the ability to bypass geographic restrictions. Many streaming services, such as Netflix and Hulu, block international users from accessing their content. With a VPN, these users can connect to a server located in a country where the service is available, thus bypassing the geographic restrictions.

Finally, a VPN is useful for those wishing to access corporate networks from outside the office. By connecting to a VPN, these users can access the corporate network as if they were connected to the local network. This is often done for security reasons, as it prevents data from being exposed to malicious actors on the public internet.
